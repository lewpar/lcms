Classic Vanilla Ice Cream

Recipes ⏱ 1 min read
Classic Vanilla Ice Cream
A French-style custard ice cream with a deep vanilla flavour and incredibly smooth, creamy texture. Requires an ice cream machine.
Makes approximately 1 litre (6–8 servings)
Ingredients
  • 300 mldouble cream (heavy cream)
  • 300 mlwhole milk
  • 5egg yolkslarge
  • 150 gcaster sugar
  • 2 tspvanilla bean pasteor the seeds of 1 vanilla pod
  • 1 pinchfine salt

1. Method#

Instructions
  1. 1
    Make the custard base

    Pour the milk and cream into a medium saucepan. Add the vanilla bean paste and a pinch of salt. Heat over medium heat until just below simmering — small bubbles should appear at the edges. Do not boil.

  2. 2
    Whisk the yolks and sugar

    In a large bowl, whisk the egg yolks and caster sugar together until the mixture is pale and slightly thickened — about 2 minutes.

  3. 3
    Temper the eggs

    Slowly pour about a third of the hot cream mixture into the egg mixture, whisking constantly. This gradually raises the temperature of the eggs without scrambling them. Pour the tempered egg mixture back into the saucepan.

  4. 4
    Cook until thickened

    Cook over low heat, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon or spatula, until the custard coats the back of the spoon and reaches 75–80°C (167–176°F). Do not let it boil.

  5. 5
    Strain and chill

    Pour the custard through a fine mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Cover the surface directly with cling film (plastic wrap) to prevent a skin forming. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight.

  6. 6
    Churn and freeze

    Pour the chilled custard into your ice cream machine and churn according to the manufacturer's instructions — usually 20–30 minutes. Transfer to a freezer-safe container and freeze for at least 2 hours before serving.

Soften before serving

Remove the ice cream from the freezer 5–10 minutes before serving. This allows it to soften slightly, making it much easier to scoop and improving the texture.

Storage

Store in an airtight container in the freezer for up to 2 weeks. Press cling film directly onto the surface before sealing to prevent ice crystals forming on top.
