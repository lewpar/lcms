Equipment & Ingredients

The Basics ⏱ 2 min read

You don't need a lot to make good ice cream, but having the right tools and understanding your ingredients will make the process much smoother.

2. Key Ingredients#

Ice cream is built on a small number of core ingredients — but the quality and ratios of those ingredients matter a great deal.

  • Double cream (heavy cream) — The high fat content (36%+) gives ice cream its rich, creamy texture and prevents large ice crystals from forming.
  • Whole milk — Adds body and a lighter texture. Skimmed milk will make the ice cream icier.
  • Egg yolks — Used in custard-based (French-style) ice cream. They emulsify the mixture and add richness.
  • Caster sugar (superfine sugar) — Dissolves easily and lowers the freezing point, keeping the ice cream scoopable.
  • Flavourings — Vanilla bean paste, cocoa powder, fruit purees, extracts, and more.
Use the fattiest cream you can find

The fat content of your cream is the single biggest factor in texture. Double cream or heavy whipping cream (36%+ fat) will give you a scoopable, smooth result. Low-fat alternatives produce an icy, hard texture.
